# Eskil

## Hook

A Norseman locked into his own armor so he cannot eat himself, walking south because the thing that wants him is still walking north.

## Identity

Eskil of Eysýsla — Varangian, Rus by trade route and tongue, six feet eight inches of scarred muscle under shaggy black hair and pale blue eyes. A steel collar is fused at his throat and a many-holed helm caged to it; the seams were welded with molten iron and have not opened since. When he can afford the materials he stains his skin red with henna and draws layered protections across his flesh — Norse warding runes from his brother Leif, elder signs from forbidden books, inverted sigils of the Black Goat. At his belt hangs an iron skull that whispers in a dead man's voice. In his hand, until the end of Book One, is a heavy northern war-blade inscribed with a Latin prayer: *A furore Normannorum libera nos, Domine.*

## Origin in brief

In the Undercroft beneath Saaremaa, the priestesses of Shub-Niggurath fed their Messenger five nights a week. Eskil was the chosen meat — drugged, bedded, and almost torn apart, his blood already half-prepared as a vessel for the concentrated vigor of his entire people. At the last instant he scratched a rune Leif had taught him, and his brother died in his place; the village burned under a purple wound in the sky and the compact of Eysýsla collapsed. What survived was Eskil, his blood now ambrosia to anything from beyond the veil, the hunger to consume himself riding inside him forever, and a fragment of Leif sealed in iron to keep him company.

## Role in the party

Eskil is the heaviest hand and the closest to the cosmic — the one the others put at the back when they walk into the dark, the one they shout at when something has its hooks in him. He stands in the line beside Bansi, lends his strength to her cleverness, and treats her with the wary respect of one survivor for another. Lüèduó he reads as kin: another man with something coiled inside that is not him, another man who knows the architecture of being ridden. With Giovanni the bond is closest and strangest — they save each other repeatedly from compulsion, the priest's voice the one that can crack through when nothing else will. At the end of Episode VII, when no craft can break the cursed runesword taken from Revna and no one else can be trusted to carry it, Giovanni claims it as penance and Eskil forges him a runed baldric to muffle its whisper. The blade passes from the man already touched by horror to the man learning what he might become — a transfer that defines what each of them carries into Book Two.

## Arc through the journey

Episode I: he intervenes in a Samarkand street to stop a public murder and joins Hind's caravan; he absorbs Rahzalahn's grief at his people without flinching. Episode II: at Merv he keeps watch when others would not, kills the werewolf in the night market, descends into the bone chapel and trades blows with Drekhlo, taking a wound the runes on his collar deflect. Episode III: at the kurgan his wards stab him before he sees the threat, marking him as the party's lodestone for the outer dark. Episode IV: in Shiraz he hunts a cell of Goat-cultists alone and rescues Lod — another marked Meat — and forges him a collar and helm of his own, teaching him the henna runes; in the Library of the Dead his blade takes a vampire's head clean. Episode V: under the accursed sands he is the one Akivasha's compulsion seizes, the one Bansi and Giovanni have to drag back from a bubbling pool by force; he scoops the Book of Eibon as he flees and chains it to himself for the voyage to Constantinople. Episode VI: in the Order of the Last Flame he reads a Nightwolf blade as one Northman reads another. Episode VII: in the Cathedral of Bones he duels Drekhlo to the killing blow, takes a greatsword through the spine and watches his own flesh knit shut with worms; he beheads Karishkir, breaks Revna's dagger, fails to break Revna's sword, and forges the baldric that lets Giovanni carry what Eskil cannot. By stages he is consumed not by hunger but by knowledge — of what marked him, of what is coming, of what he is becoming useful for.

## Thematic note

Eskil is the man who can stand in front of horror because horror has already eaten part of him. He is the party's proof that the cosmic does not always kill on first contact — sometimes it marks, and the marked walk on, and their walking becomes shelter for everyone behind them. The cost is that he can never be a man among men again: he sleeps apart, eats alone, locks his own mouth shut, and lets the brother in the iron skull keep him sane. His refusal to carry the runesword at the end is not weakness; it is the clearest self-knowledge in the company. He understands, before any of them, that the things that touched him are still reaching, and that the work of standing between them and the world is the only work left for someone built like him.
